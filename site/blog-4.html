Why we run paid media inside the brand engagement, not separately.

Splitting brand and performance into two vendors is the most common $400K mistake we see. The brand agency builds the site. The performance agency runs ads that look like nobody read the brand book. Both vendors blame the other when the funnel underperforms.

For most of our clients, paid media is the first surface a prospect sees of the brand. A LinkedIn ad in the feed, a Google search result, a YouTube pre-roll. The ad is not a separate artefact from the brand — it is the brand, at the moment of first impression. Treating it as a separate vendor's problem is a category error.

The creative gap is where the money leaks.

The standard performance agency runs on creative volume — 40 to 80 ad variants a month, churned out by junior designers who never spoke to the brand team. The result: a feed of ads that share none of the typographic, motion or photographic discipline of the site they lead to. Click-through goes up modestly. Brand-lift surveys collapse. CAC eventually rises because the audience has stopped trusting the brand they're being shown.

When the same studio runs both, the ad creative inherits the design system. Type, colour, motion principles, photography direction — all aligned. The CPM is the same. The brand-lift is materially different.

An ad that doesn't look like your site is a leak. You're paying to introduce two different companies to the same customer.

Attribution is a brand question, not a tracking question.

The classical performance shop treats attribution as a tracking problem — pixels, UTMs, conversion APIs. We treat it as a brand-narrative problem. We instrument GA4 and server-side events because we must, but the questions we actually answer are: which message landed? Which audience-statement converted? Which positioning claim moved the demo-request rate? Those are brand questions, dressed up as performance reports.

The honest part.

This isn't right for every company. If you're spending $5M a month on Meta and Google, you probably want a specialist performance shop with a 15-person team. If you're spending $40K to $400K a month and you want every impression to compound the brand, the integrated model wins. Most companies we work with are in the second bucket, by a wide margin.

We run paid media as part of the brand engagement because the brand is the asset. Performance is the way the asset earns. Splitting them is the mistake.

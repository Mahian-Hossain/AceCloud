The challenge

Halycon Capital had been operating for 26 years but their website looked like it was built in 2014. The design communicated "regional PE shop" when the reality was a $4B+ fund with a category-defining thesis on infrastructure capital.

They were six months from the biggest fundraise in their history. LPs were landing on a site that undercut every in-person conversation. The brief: make the site worthy of the conversation, without losing the gravitas that 26 years earns you.

What we did

We started with a positioning workshop with the founding partners. The key insight: Halycon's differentiation wasn't returns — it was patience. A 10-year hold horizon is genuinely rare in PE. The new positioning: "patient infrastructure capital."

From that brief we designed a site that felt like a Bloomberg terminal crossed with a serious print publication — dark, editorial, credible. The typography system uses a mix of display Geist and editorial Instrument Serif for emphasis. Every section has a clear job in the LP conversion funnel.

Built on Next.js with Sanity CMS in eight weeks, including brand system, copy and development.

The process

Eight weeks, four clear phases.

We compressed a typical 16-week brand and site project into eight weeks by running brand and design simultaneously after the positioning brief was locked. The critical dependency was the written positioning brief — once that was approved, everything else was parallel work.

Week 1–2 · Positioning

Stakeholder interviews with four founding partners. Competitor teardown of 12 comparable funds. Written positioning brief: "Patient infrastructure capital for operators building the next decade of industrial technology."

Week 2–4 · Brand + Design

Visual identity: dark editorial direction with Instrument Serif for editorial emphasis and Geist for data and navigation. Site architecture: 7 pages, all designed simultaneously. Copy written by our team with partner review.

Week 4–7 · Build

Next.js 14, Sanity CMS with a custom block builder for the team's editorial needs. Cloudflare deployment. 97 Lighthouse average.

Week 7–8 · Launch

QA, accessibility audit, analytics setup (GA4 + Hotjar) and a pre-launch press brief. Launched six weeks before the fund close.

The challenge

Quanta Labs had two problems. First: their technology was genuinely ahead of the market — 4× inference speed at 3× lower cost — but their website made them look like an early-stage experiment. Second: their potential customers (AI engineers at Series B+ companies) were deeply sceptical of benchmark claims. Every competitor made the same promises.

The brief required both a credibility problem and a differentiation problem to be solved simultaneously. The site needed to be proof, not promise — with real benchmark data, real architecture documentation, and a brand that felt like it had been built by people who understood how production AI actually works.

Our approach

We built the benchmark visualisation as a core design element, not an afterthought. The homepage leads with real data — latency comparisons against AWS, Azure, GCP, and Together AI across five model families. Every number is independently verifiable; we linked directly to the methodology.

The visual language draws from semiconductor manufacturing and systems architecture: dark backgrounds with precise grid overlays, electric violet accents for interactive elements, and Geist Mono used heavily because it signals "engineers built this." The brand mark is a stylised quantum circuit symbol — distinctive but unpretentious.

Built on Next.js with a custom benchmark visualisation system using D3.js, full documentation architecture in MDX, and a request-access flow integrated with their CRM.

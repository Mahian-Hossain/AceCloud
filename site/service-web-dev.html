How we work

Ship behind a flag. Reveal progressively.

We ship in two-week sprints and deploy behind feature flags so you can review in production before anything goes live to users.

Week 1–2 · Technical discovery

Stack audit, CMS requirements, integration inventory (analytics, CRM, auth), performance baseline on existing site and a technical architecture brief. We agree on the stack before we write a line of code.

Week 2–6 · Build

Component library first, then pages. Continuous deployment to a preview environment so you're reviewing in a real browser, not a Figma prototype. CMS structure built alongside the components it powers.

Week 6–8 · QA & launch

Cross-browser QA, accessibility audit (WCAG 2.1 AA), performance audit, security review and a pre-launch checklist. Launch is coordinated with your team so it happens when you're ready, not when we're done.

Post-launch support

60-day hypercare window: we monitor uptime, Core Web Vitals and error rates. Bug fixes within 24 hours. We also run a 30-day retrospective and hand over a documented runbook for your team.

Questions

Common questions answered honestly.

Why Next.js and not Webflow, Framer or WordPress?

For simple marketing sites with a content team, Webflow or Framer is often the right call and we'll say so. Next.js is the right choice when you need performance at scale, programmatic page generation, a custom design system, complex integrations or a site that will live inside a larger product architecture. We don't have a preferred stack — we have the right stack for your situation.

Can you take over a site someone else built?

Yes, though we do a technical audit first to understand what we're inheriting. We'll give you an honest assessment of the existing codebase, identify what's worth keeping and what needs replacing, and scope the engagement from there. We don't charge for the audit.

Do you provide hosting?

We set up and configure Vercel or Cloudflare deployments, but the hosting account is in your name from day one. We don't hold your infrastructure hostage. After launch, your team controls the deployment pipeline and we document everything so you can operate it independently.

What CMS do you recommend?

Sanity for most cases — it's flexible, developer-friendly and has the best real-time preview experience. Contentful if you need enterprise-grade permissions and a well-established workflow. Payload if you want a self-hosted, code-first option. We're opinionated but not dogmatic — we'll pick the one that fits your team's actual content workflow.

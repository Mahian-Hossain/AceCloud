The challenge

Fieldnotes had a loyal following on Instagram and a product that people genuinely loved. But their website was a basic Shopify template with stock photography that could have been selling anything. The conversion rate reflected it — 0.8% on qualified traffic that was clearly interested enough to click through from social.

The founder had a clear vision for the brand but no way to translate it into digital. Products that retail at a premium need a site that earns that price point. Theirs didn't.

Our approach

We started with a brand foundation session — tone of voice, visual identity, photography direction, and a typography system that felt analog even on screen. Instrument Serif for headlines, Geist Mono for product metadata. Warm, off-white backgrounds with deep brown and ochre as accent.

The site was rebuilt on a custom Shopify theme with a headless storefront layer for performance. Every product page was designed to tell a story — materials, origin, use case — alongside the standard add-to-cart flow. We also implemented a subscription model for their core memo books, which became their highest-margin revenue channel within two months.

The process

Ten weeks from positioning to a live store.

Brand and e-commerce projects have a natural dependency — the brand has to be right before the site can be designed. We compress this by running brand and component design in parallel after the core visual direction is locked, typically at the end of week three.

Week 1–2 · Brand foundation

Competitor analysis of 20 premium stationery brands. Positioning brief: "the memo book for people who think clearly." Visual direction: warm analog, dark editorial, obsessively precise.

Week 2–5 · Identity + design

Logo, type system, colour palette, photography art direction. Site architecture: homepage, collections, product pages, about, journal. All 18 screens designed in Figma.

Week 5–9 · Build

Custom Shopify theme with metafields for product storytelling. Subscription logic via Recharge. Performance: 94 Lighthouse average, sub-1.5s LCP.

Week 9–10 · Launch

Product photography review, copy finalisation, email flow setup (welcome, abandon cart, post-purchase), GA4 + Meta Pixel, and a launch week paid social campaign.

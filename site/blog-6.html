A 95+ Lighthouse score is a brand decision.

Performance budgets are how we keep the marketing team's "one more video" instinct from breaking the trust we just spent twelve weeks building.

Performance is treated as an engineering hygiene metric in most companies. We treat it as a brand hygiene metric. The first impression of any brand on the web is the half-second between click and first paint. If that half-second turns into three seconds, the brand impression is "slow, unserious, expensive". You can hand-letter the wordmark for nine months and lose that impression in three seconds of loading.

What we enforce.

Every page we ship is gated in CI on Lighthouse 95+ across performance, accessibility, best-practices and SEO. We enforce a per-page JavaScript budget of 170KB gzipped. Hero images are width-served, AVIF first, JPEG fallback. We refuse autoplay video on landing pages — it's almost always a lose-lose. We pre-render everything that can be pre-rendered and we use edge ISR for the rest. None of this is heroic engineering. It's a small set of refusals, applied consistently.

Performance is the only brand attribute the customer measures unconsciously. You can fake the headline. You can't fake the first paint.

The conversations that get easier.

When performance is a published budget rather than a vague intention, the conversation with marketing becomes finite. "We'd love a 12MB scroll-triggered video on the homepage" gets a one-sentence answer: yes, if we cut something else, here's what. It is not a moral debate. It is a budget.

The same conversation is impossible to have without a budget. The video gets added. Then the chat widget. Then the heat-mapper. Then the four-vendor analytics stack. Six months later the page weighs 8MB and nobody can remember the day it broke.

What scoring 95+ buys you.

Two things, mostly. First, the visceral feeling of a site that is fast and polite, which we can't quantify but the customer registers immediately. Second, every downstream SEO and AI-visibility surface that uses page-experience as a ranking input — Google classical, AI Overviews, LLM crawlers that down-rank slow pages. The 95+ score is not vanity. It is a small but meaningful lever on the whole demand surface.

It is also a refusal we make on behalf of the brand, against everyone in the company who'd quietly prefer a slower site with one more thing on it. The role of the studio is to keep saying no.

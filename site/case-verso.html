The challenge

Verso's founding editor had previously run technology coverage at a national newspaper. She understood exactly what quality editorial looked like — and Ghost, the platform they'd launched on, couldn't support the publication infrastructure she needed. Reader payments, individual article paywalls, per-writer analytics, newsletter integration, and a reading experience worthy of the writing.

The technical challenge was significant: a custom CMS with role-based access for five writers, a subscription layer (Stripe) with metered article access, an email delivery pipeline, and a search function across 200+ archived pieces — all in 14 weeks.

Our approach

We designed Verso to feel like a serious print publication on screen. The reading experience prioritises the words: generous margins, a line-length capped at 72 characters, Instrument Serif for editorial type and Geist for everything navigational. Dark mode available but light is primary — because the writing is meant to be read in the morning with coffee.

The content architecture was built around editorial intent, not algorithmic priority. The homepage is a hand-curated front page, not an algorithmic feed. Articles can be marked "Free", "Metered" (3 free per month), or "Subscribers only" — the editor controls the business model at the piece level.

Built on Next.js with Sanity as the headless CMS, Stripe for subscriptions, Loops for email, and Meilisearch for full-text archive search. Deployed on Vercel with ISR for article pages.

"We moved from Ghost to a proper publication stack and didn't lose a single subscriber in migration. The reading experience is now as good as anything in the industry. It respects the reader's attention."
Nadia Laurent · Editor in Chief, Verso

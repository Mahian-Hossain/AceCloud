Redesigning a marketplace from the checkout back.

Mercato connects independent grocers with local shoppers. The product worked. The UX didn't — customers were dropping out at checkout, and support was overwhelmed with questions the interface should have answered. We redesigned the purchase flow and merchant dashboard from first principles, and the numbers moved fast.

The challenge

Mercato had strong PMF — independent grocer inventory, same-day delivery, genuinely better produce than supermarkets. But their checkout abandoned 66% of users. Session recordings showed the same patterns: confusion around delivery windows, ambiguity about substitution preferences, and a payment step that felt insecure even though it wasn't.

The merchant dashboard had a parallel problem. Grocers were calling support to ask questions that the dashboard answered poorly — inventory sync issues, order status ambiguity, payout timing. Support tickets were growing faster than the business.

Our approach

We started by spending a week with the support team, cataloguing the 40 most common questions. Every support ticket is a design failure. The list became the product backlog.

The checkout redesign focused on three things: progressive disclosure (show delivery options only after the cart is confirmed), explicit substitution preference (ask once, remember always), and a payment step that felt like it belonged to a trusted brand. We ran two rounds of unmoderated user testing before writing a line of production code.

The merchant dashboard was redesigned around daily job flows — opening inventory, processing orders, reconciling payouts. Every screen has one primary action and a clear next step.

How we work

Six weeks, clear gates, no surprises.

We run web design projects in two-week sprints with a written brief at every gate. You always know what's coming next and why.

Week 1–2 · Discovery

Stakeholder interviews, competitor audit, user journey mapping, ICP review and content inventory. Output: a written creative brief covering positioning, narrative structure, tone and a prioritised page list. You sign off before we open Figma.

Week 2–4 · Design

Homepage first, then the design system, then all remaining pages. Two rounds of feedback built into the schedule. We design in Figma with shared access so you can comment in real time — no waiting for a formal review deck.

Week 4–6 · Polish & handoff

Motion design, micro-interactions, responsive breakpoints and accessibility review. Developer handoff package includes a component inventory, spacing specs and interaction notes. If we're also building it, this phase transitions directly to engineering.

Post-launch

30-day support window after launch. We monitor analytics, fix anything that slips through QA, and make minor copy and layout adjustments as you see real-world user behaviour.

Questions

What people actually ask us.

Do you design in Webflow, Framer or custom code?

Design is always in Figma. For the build, it depends on your situation. Simple marketing sites with a content team usually benefit from Webflow or Framer. Complex, performance-critical sites go Next.js. We recommend the right tool for your needs, not the one we're most comfortable billing for.

Can you work with our existing brand?

Yes. We frequently design within an existing brand system. We'll audit your brand assets first and flag any issues — gaps in the system, inconsistent usage, missing components — before we start designing pages.

We have a developer already. Can you just do design?

Absolutely. We deliver developer-ready Figma files with full component specs, interaction notes and responsive variants. We're also happy to have a handoff call with your dev team to walk through the design decisions.

How many revision rounds do we get?

Two structured rounds per phase, plus an open round at final review. In practice, projects move faster than this because we write a detailed brief before we design — so revisions tend to be refinements, not redirections. We don't put a hard cap on iterations for clients we're in an ongoing retainer with.

What's your process for mobile design?

We design mobile and desktop simultaneously, not as an afterthought. Components are designed at their most constrained breakpoint first, then expanded to desktop. Every deliverable includes mobile, tablet and desktop variants.

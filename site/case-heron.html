The challenge

Heron Health's platform was clinically leading. Their AI triage and care coordination tools were being used at 40+ clinics. But the patient-facing interface — the portal patients used to book, prepare for and follow up after appointments — was inherited from a 2018 acquisition and hadn't been touched since.

The consequences were measurable: 62% appointment abandonment, 18% no-show rate, and patient satisfaction scores averaging 3.1/5. The clinical team was doing great work that was being undermined by a confusing portal patients couldn't use.

Our approach

We started with three weeks of user research — shadowing at 4 clinic locations, 22 patient interviews across age groups (18–72), and heatmap analysis of the existing portal. The core problem was clear: patients didn't trust the system. Every UI element communicated "institutional" rather than "caring."

The design system we built draws from consumer health apps — calm, trustworthy blue palette, clear information hierarchy, plain English everywhere (no medical jargon in navigation labels), and proactive status communication at every step.

The booking flow was rebuilt as a 4-step wizard, down from an 11-step form. Confirmation emails, calendar links, and 24-hour reminder logic were woven into the system from the start.

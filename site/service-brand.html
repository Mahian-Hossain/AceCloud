How we work

Four weeks to a brand that holds.

We run brand work in tight two-week cycles with written briefs at every gate. You see the thinking before you see the design.

Week 1–2 · Diagnose

Brand audit, stakeholder interviews, competitor teardown and ICP research. Output: a written positioning brief you approve before we open Figma. This is the most important stage and most agencies skip it.

Week 2–4 · Design

Identity concepts developed in three directions. Each direction is built from the brief — not picked from a mood board. We present rationale, not just work. One direction is refined to final.

Week 4–6 · System

Approved identity extended into a full system — type hierarchy, spacing grid, component library, photography style and motion principles. Delivered as a Figma file with shared library access.

Week 6–8 · Handoff

Live guidelines site or Notion doc. Handoff session with your team. 30-day post-launch support window so we're available when you hit your first real-world edge case.

Questions

Honest answers.

We already have a logo. Do we need a full brand system?

Depends. A logo alone doesn't give your product team, sales team and social team a consistent way to represent the company. If you're finding that every new asset looks slightly different, or that your team has to guess at colours, fonts and layouts — you need a system. We can audit what you have and extend it, or start fresh if it's beyond salvaging.

How involved does our team need to be?

We need two to three hours in week one for a brand workshop and stakeholder interviews. After that, feedback rounds are async and time-boxed to 48 hours. We don't run endless approval cycles. Each gate has a written brief, and we move forward once it's approved. Total time commitment for your team: roughly 8–10 hours over six weeks.

What if we don't like the direction?

We present three distinct directions at concept stage, each built from your approved brief. If none land, we have a revision round built into the engagement. In practice, this has never happened — because we write the brief first and get alignment before we design. The brief is the contract.

Do you work with in-house designers?

Yes, and we prefer it. An engaged in-house designer shortens feedback loops and produces better outcomes. We build the Figma library with shared library access and document every decision so your team can extend the system independently after handoff.

What does $18K get us exactly?

At the $18K entry point: positioning brief, wordmark, type system, colour palette, and a component library in Figma with brand guidelines. Larger engagements (rebrand + site) typically run $32K–$55K depending on scope. We scope every project in writing before you commit to anything.

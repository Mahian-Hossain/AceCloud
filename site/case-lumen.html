The challenge

Lumen Bio's founding team were world-class synthetic biologists. Their work on engineered microorganisms for carbon capture had attracted attention from the DOE and three top-tier research institutions. But when climate tech VCs visited their site, they found a white background, Times New Roman headings, and a PDF of their research paper as the main CTA.

The brief arrived six weeks before a key investor dinner. They needed a brand that communicated both scientific rigour and commercial urgency — a combination that most biotech brands get completely wrong by leaning too far toward either the laboratory or the pitch deck.

Our approach

The insight that shaped everything: bioluminescence. Lumen's microorganisms emit light as a byproduct of carbon sequestration — it's how you measure their activity. "Light from living systems" became the brand platform, and it gave us a visual language that was immediately distinctive: deep forest green backgrounds, bioluminescent greens, Instrument Serif for the science narrative and Geist for the data.

The site has one job: make a technically sophisticated investor understand the mechanism and the market opportunity in five minutes. We structured the content as a three-act narrative — what the problem is, how Lumen's biology solves it, why this team and this moment — with supporting data on sequestration rates, cost curves, and IP position throughout.

What we got right

Science brands need narrative, not just data.

Most biotech sites make the same mistake: they lead with mechanism and bury the market. Investors aren't scientists — they need the "so what" before the "how." The Lumen site leads with the climate problem, introduces the biology as the surprising solution, and then delivers the technical proof for the readers who want it.

The illustration system we designed was important for the same reason. Abstract scientific concepts become memorable with the right visual metaphor. We worked with Lumen's researchers over two weeks to develop illustrations that were scientifically accurate but visually clear. Not dumbed down — translated.

Week 1–2 · Positioning

Five stakeholder interviews with founders, board advisor, and a climate VC to understand what investors actually need to see. Written positioning brief: "the biology that makes carbon capture commercially viable."

Week 2–4 · Identity + design

Visual identity: bioluminescent dark palette. Illustration direction: microorganism diagrams, carbon cycle visualisations, impact projection charts. Site IA: 8 pages, investor-journey optimised.

Week 4–6 · Build + launch

Next.js 14 with Framer Motion for scroll animations. 98 Lighthouse. Sanity CMS so the science team could update their research section directly. Hard launch timed to investor dinner.

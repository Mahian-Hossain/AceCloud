The challenge

Modus Studio's portfolio included award-winning residential projects, a commercial fitout for a Michelin-starred Toronto restaurant, and a boutique hotel in Whistler. Their brand included none of that ambition: a geometric logomark that could have belonged to an accountancy firm, and a Flash-era portfolio structure rebuilt in Wix.

The founders came to us six months before a major pitch to a luxury condo developer. They needed a brand and a site that could sit credibly alongside agencies like Snøhetta and BHDM when the client opened multiple tabs.

Creative direction

We began with the work itself — three weeks of immersion in 40+ Modus projects, studying what made each one distinctive. The consistent thread: restraint. Modus buildings are notable for what they don't do — no unnecessary material, no gratuitous gesture. The brand needed that same economy.

The wordmark is set in a single-weight uppercase geometric sans — minimal, architecturally precise. The colour palette is drawn from the materials Modus uses: warm limestone, aged brass, dark concrete, bleached linen. The site layout prioritises the photography — full-bleed, no distraction, no competing elements.

The portfolio is structured as a numbered index that expands to full-screen imagery. Every project page is unique, built around the images rather than forced into a template.
